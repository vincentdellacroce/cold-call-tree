📞 The Opener ▶
"Hey, how's it going — did Howard from the IRS reach out to you today?"
— "No.. why?"
"Well that's good because I don't know a Howard from the IRS. I was actually calling because I came across your business and I noticed you guys don't have a website. I actually went and put together a mock-up for you, free of charge. I want to jump on a quick Google Meet at the end of the day — like 5 o'clock — pull it up, walk you through it, takes 10 minutes. If you hate it we never have to talk again."
OBJ 1 "I'm not interested" ▶
"That's totally fair — honestly I'd say the same thing if a random number called me. I'm not trying to sell you anything right now, no credit card, nothing like that. All I did was put together a quick mock-up of what your business could look like online. Takes you 10 minutes to look at it, and if you think it's garbage, you never have to hear from me again. Genuinely. I just figured — you're out there doing good work, and nobody in the tri-county area can even find you online. That felt wrong. So I fixed it, at least on paper. Can we just jump on a quick Google Meet at end of day — like 5 o'clock?"
If they say no to the meet
"Can I ask why? Like genuinely — is it a time thing, or you just don't see the point of a website for your business?"
A "I don't see the point / don't need a website" ▶
"I hear that a lot actually. Can I ask — how do most of your jobs come in right now? Referrals?"
They say yes.
"Yeah that's how most guys in the trades run it, and honestly referrals are great. But here's what I've seen with landscapers in the area — the referral guys who also have a clean site are charging 20-30% more because they look established. The customer already Googled them before they even called. You're doing the same work, just getting paid less for it because on paper you don't exist yet. That's really all I'm trying to fix for you. 10 minutes on a Google Meet, I'll show you what it looks like. If it doesn't change your mind, I'll leave you alone forever. What does 5 o'clock look like?"
Still no.
"Alright — what time works better for you? Morning, afternoon, I'll work around you completely."
Still no.
"You know what, I respect it. Let me ask you one thing before I let you go — if you woke up tomorrow and your phone was ringing off the hook from people in the area finding you on Google, would that be a problem for your business or a good thing?"
They say "good thing".
"That's all a website does. I'll tell you what — I'm not going to waste your time with a meeting. Let me just text you a link later today. You look at it on your own time, no call, no pressure. If you want to talk you text me back, if not, no hard feelings. What's the best number to text it to?"
Refuses to give number / still no.
"Totally fine. I've built sites for landscapers in the area, both from scratch and for guys who already had something. Every single one of them said the same thing you're saying right now before they saw it. I'm not saying that to pressure you. I'm saying it because I get it, and I also know what's on the other side of 10 minutes. If you ever change your mind, my name is [Name]. You can find me at [business name]. I'll leave you alone now — but seriously, good luck out there."
Hang up gracefully — they may call back.
B "I already have someone / a website" ▶
"Oh nice — where's the site at? I'll take a look real quick while I have you."
"No worries — I'll find it. But let me ask, are you happy with the leads it's bringing in, or is it more just kind of there?"
They say it's just there / not getting leads.
"Yeah that's actually the most common thing I hear. A lot of guys have a site but it's not really working — it's not showing up on Google, it doesn't look great on mobile, nobody's filling out the contact form. A site that doesn't bring in calls is basically just a business card nobody looks at. What I put together for you is built specifically to rank in the tri-county area and actually convert visitors into calls. That's the difference. 10 minutes on a Google Meet and I'll show you exactly what I mean — you can literally compare them side by side. What does 5 o'clock look like?"
"I'm happy with what I have."
"That's great honestly — most guys aren't. Can I ask, are you getting consistent leads from it or is it more word of mouth still?"
Word of mouth still.
"So the site exists but it's not really doing the heavy lifting yet. That's exactly what I specialize in. I'm not asking you to scrap what you have — I'm just saying let me show you what a site that actually brings in calls looks like, side by side with yours. 10 minutes. If yours is already doing everything mine does, I'll tell you straight up and leave you alone. Fair?"
Still no → go to the "text you a link" move from Branch A.

"Totally get it — what's a better time? I'll call you back whenever works, morning, evening, weekend, I don't care."
They give a time — lock it in.
"Perfect. I'll call you at [time] on [day]. What's the best number to reach you on so I don't come up as a random number again?"
Won't commit to a time.
"Got it. I'll try you again [tomorrow morning / end of week]. And in the meantime I'll text you the mock-up so you can get a feel for it before we even talk. Sound good?"

But let's not even go there yet. Let me just show you the mock-up on a Google Meet at 5 — if you love it, we talk numbers. If not, zero obligation. Does that work?"
